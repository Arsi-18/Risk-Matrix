Risk Matrix Tool

A structured tool for risk assessment and management.

What is a Risk Matrix?

A Risk Matrix is a structured tool used in risk management to evaluate and prioritize risks based on their likelihood and severity.

Practical Example

For example, in a software project, risks like Data Loss and UI Bugs can be assessed using this tool.

Key Components of a Risk Matrix

Likelihood (Probability of Occurrence)

  • Almost Certain – Highly likely to occur.
  • Likely – Expected to happen at some point.
  • Possible – May or may not occur.
  • Rare – Unlikely to happen.

Severity (Impact of the Risk)

  • Insignificant – Minimal impact.
  • Minor – Small impact, system continues.
  • Moderate – Noticeable impact, manageable.
  • Major – Serious impact.
  • Severe (Critical) – Catastrophic impact.
